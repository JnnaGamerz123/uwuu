Note: It only contains the review of “THE SINS OF PRINCE SARADINE”.

The story of Prince Saradine takes place in an ancient and mysterious England, with Gothic overtones. There are also explicit mentions of fairyland, which Father Brown appropriately describes as macabre. The problem, in particular, a case of mistaken identity that, as often happens with this type of story, leaves a line of loose ends. According to me it was an ok story.

This review was about story now about the book. At the first page we can see a sword. This same sword can be seen on the bottom right corner from page number 6 to all the even numbers. It gives an animation effect if we use it as a flipbook.

This is absoulutely amazing!!!
